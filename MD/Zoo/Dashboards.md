# Dashboards

## Phase 1.5 – Core Dashboards (right after Phase 1)

Goal: quick, motivating overview of **consistency**, **grammar**, **fillers**, and **vocab usage** – using only Phase-1 animals.

### 1️⃣ Dashboard: “Consistency & Time”

**Purpose:** Show how regularly you train and how much you speak.

**Data sources:**

- 🐦 ScribeSparrow – sessions, durations, types
- 🐬 DayDolphin – which days had activity

**Widgets:**

1. **Sessions per day (last 4 weeks)**
    - Bar chart: X-axis = days, Y-axis = number of sessions.
    - Color code:
        - Dark bar = at least 1 focused session (quick/full)
        - Light bar = only free conversation
2. **Speaking time per day**
    - Line or bar chart: minutes of active speaking per day.
    - Rough categories:
        - < 5 min (micro)
        - 5–20 min (normal)
        - 20 min (long)
3. **Streak indicator**
    - Simple: “You trained on X of the last 7 days”
    - Nice warm summary like: *“Solid: 5/7 days with at least one session.”*

---

### 2️⃣ Dashboard: “Grammar & Fillers Trend”

**Purpose:** Track whether your language is getting cleaner and smoother.

**Data sources:**

- 🦒 GrammarGiraffe – counts of grammar issues per type
- 🦅 FillerFalcon – filler tokens & rate
- 🐯 TempoTiger (optional) – WPM and average pause length

**Widgets:**

1. **Grammar issues per 100 words (daily/weekly)**
    - Line chart.
    - Optionally split into:
        - articles, tenses, prepositions, word order (stacked bars or grouped).
2. **Filler rate trend**
    - Line chart: % of words that are fillers over time.
    - For example:
        - 12% → 9% → 7% …
    - Good for seeing progress even when you don’t *feel* it.
3. **Fluency indicator (optional)**
    - WPM and median pause duration per session.
    - Just a simple box:
        - “Typical pace: 110–130 WPM, median pause: 0.6 s”

---

### 3️⃣ Dashboard: “Vocabulary & Notion Activity”

**Purpose:** Show how your **Notion vocabulary** is being used and reviewed.

**Data sources:**

- 🐈‍⬛ LexiLynx – when a Notion item is heard/used
- 🐦 NotionNightingale – Notion IDs + categories
- 🐿️ SpacedSquirrel – review events & difficulty

**Widgets:**

1. **Active vs passive vocab (per week)**
    - Two numbers:
        - **Seen**: Notion items that appeared in prompts / suggestions.
        - **Used**: Notion items you actually used in your own utterances.
    - Bonus: a ratio (“Usage: 18 used / 42 seen”).
2. **Top 10 “alive” Notion items**
    - Table with:
        - Expression
        - Times used correctly
        - Last used date
        - Category (e.g. Communication Verbs)
3. **Sleeping items (dead wood)**
    - List of items:
        - present in Notion
        - but not used for X days (e.g. 30+).
    - Good shortlist for future review sessions.
4. **Review stats (Spaced repetition)**
    - Cards showing:
        - Items reviewed today / this week
        - Approximate success rate (e.g. “72% easy, 28% struggled”)

---

### 4️⃣ Dashboard: “Session Review (Last Session View)”

**Purpose:** One-page summary of the **last completed session**.

**Data sources:**

- 🐦 ScribeSparrow – drills vs free talk, focus used
- 🐦 GrammarGiraffe + 🦅 FillerFalcon – main issues worked on
- 🐈‍⬛ LexiLynx – vocab items practiced

**Widgets:**

1. **Timeline of the last session**
    - Simple list or mini-timeline:
        - 0–3 min: Warm-up (topic: X)
        - 3–10 min: Grammar focus (articles)
        - 10–15 min: Vocab from Notion (items: X, Y, Z)
    - Mark drills as ✅ completed / ⚪ skipped.
2. **Key focus summary**
    - “Main focus: fillers & article errors”
    - “2 collocations practiced: ‘refine the material’, ‘get the message across’”
3. **Small text summary for you**
    - Generated by CoachCoyote, but fed by logs, e.g.:
        
        > “Today you reduced fillers slightly, and you practised 2 expressions from your Notion list. Next time, we can build on the same topic or switch focus to phrasal verbs.”
        >

## Phase 3 – Advanced Dashboards (after Phase 2 is in place)

Goal: Add **depth**: listening, pronunciation, actors, emotions, strategies, long-term skill profile.

### 1️⃣ Dashboard: “Skill Radar”

**Purpose:** High-level **skill profile** view.

**Data sources:**

- 🦒 GrammarGiraffe – grammar error rate
- 🦅 EchoEagle – lexical variety & overuse
- 🐯 TempoTiger + 🐧 PronunciationPenguin – fluency & pronunciation
- 🐆 ComprehensionCougar – listening scores
- 🦢 StrategySwan – strategy use (how often you try paraphrasing, chunking etc.)

**Widgets:**

1. **Skill radar chart**
    - Axes:
        - Grammar accuracy
        - Lexical precision & variety
        - Fluency (pace & pauses)
        - Pronunciation clarity
        - Listening comprehension
        - Strategy use (how often strategies are applied)
2. **Progress snapshots**
    - Compare:
        - “Now” vs “30 days ago” radar outlines.
    - Great for seeing macro-progress.

---

### 2️⃣ Dashboard: “Pronunciation & Prosody”

**Purpose:** Track pronunciation & rhythm improvements.

**Data sources:**

- 🐧 PronunciationPenguin – mispronunciation/stress flags
- 🐯 TempoTiger – timing, chunking, pauses
- 🕊️ ActorAlbatross – shadowing attempts & success

**Widgets:**

1. **Words frequently misheard / corrected**
    - List of top problem words:
        - e.g. “third / world / comfortable / focus”
        - each with a trend (issues per week).
2. **Shadowing activity**
    - Number of actor lines shadowed this week.
    - Optional: % of lines where rhythm & stress were “acceptable” (rough metric).
3. **Chunking & pause patterns**
    - Before/after distributions:
        - average phrase length (in words)
        - average pause between chunks

---

### 3️⃣ Dashboard: “Listening & Actors”

**Purpose:** Show progress in listening tasks and learned actor phrases.

**Data sources:**

- 🐆 ComprehensionCougar – listening task results
- 🕷️ ScriptSpider + 🦀 CrawlerCrab – source clips
- 🕊️ ActorAlbatross – actor phrases mined & practised

**Widgets:**

1. **Listening task performance**
    - Score per task type:
        - comprehension questions
        - “gist summary”
        - keyword dictation
    - Trend over time.
2. **Actor activity**
    - Which actors you trained with
    - Number of clips per actor
    - Favourite actor phrases learned (expressions with high correct-usage count)
3. **Real-life phrase adoption**
    - Phrases originally from actors now used spontaneously in your speech.

---

### 4️⃣ Dashboard: “Emotion & Load”

**Purpose:** Detect overload vs healthy challenge.

**Data sources:**

- 🐘 EmpathyElephant – skipped drills, short answers, self-critical expressions
- 🐼 PersonaPanda – mode, your preferences
- 🐦 ScribeSparrow – sessions & intensity

**Widgets:**

1. **Drill acceptance vs skipping**
    - Chart of:
        - drills offered vs completed vs skipped.
    - If skipping climbs → maybe we’re too intense.
2. **Self-talk monitor (light)**
    - Count of negatively framed sentences about your own ability:
        - “I’m bad at X” / “I can’t” / “This is impossible” etc.
    - Just for you, *not* judgmental.
3. **Mode usage**
    - How often you were in:
        - off / soft / normal / intense mode.
    - Use this to tune defaults with BoundaryBison.

---

### 5️⃣ Dashboard: “Strategic Learning”

**Purpose:** Show how you’re using *strategies*, not just words.

**Data sources:**

- 🦢 StrategySwan – strategy-triggered drills
- 🐦 ScribeSparrow – which sessions had strategy blocks

**Widgets:**

1. **Strategy usage count**
    - Paraphrasing drills done
    - Chunking drills
    - “Key-point first” drills
    - Listening-for-keywords drills
2. **Before/after examples list**
    - A curated small set of “before vs after” sentences where StrategySwan helped you restructure expression.
    - Very motivating to read your own language evolution.

---

## Implementation order suggestion

To keep this manageable:

- **After Phase 1 (Phase 1.5 Dashboards):**
    1. Consistency & time
    2. Grammar & fillers trend
    3. Vocabulary & Notion activity
    4. Last-session summary
- **After Phase 2 (Phase 3 Dashboards):**
    1. Skill radar
    2. Pronunciation & prosody
    3. Listening & actors
    4. Emotion & load
    5. Strategy usage
